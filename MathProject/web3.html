Introduction to Trigonometric Functions


Trigonometry's fundamental ideas center on the sine (sin), cosine (cos), and
tangent (tan) trigonometric functions, coined as SOH CAH TOA. The ratios of a
right triangle's sides serve as the foundation for the definition of these functions.


Parts of a Right Triangle



The hypotenuse is the longest side of a right triangle. It is directly opposite to
the right angle.


The adjacent is the side next to the given angle.


The opposite is the side across the given angle.


The theta (θ) represents the angle in the triangle.


Trigonometric Ratios



In addition to the trigonometric ratios, there are reciprocal functions that are
sine, cosine, and tangent's opposites, coined as CHO SHA CAO.


Reciprocal Trigonometric Ratios




Using these functions, you can find the sides of a right triangle using its angle.
