Introduction to Trigonometry


Many areas of study, most notably physics, architecture and engineering, 
use trigonometry extensively. But what is trigonometry?


Trigonometry is a branch of Mathematics that studies triangles.
It originates from the Greek terms "trigonon," which translates as "triangle," and "metron," which means "measure."


This online course provides all the basics for Trigonometry, along with useful examples,
videos and an examination at the end to test your knowledge!


1. Pythagorean Theorem.


The Pythagorean Theorem is one of the basic concepts of Trigonometry.
It is used for finding the length of the sides of a right triangle.
To understand its ideas and application, let us first introduce some of its basic concepts.

Parts of a Right Triangle



The hypothenuse is the longest side of a right triangle. It is directly opposite to the right angle.
Side a and b are the legs of a right triangle.


Examples:




Solve for x


Step 1


Square sides a and b.
c²=a²+b²
x²=6²+8²

Step 2


Add both sides.
x²=100

Step 3


Square root the sum.


√x²=√100


x=10



Solve for y


Step 1


Square the hypothenuse and leg.


c²=a²+b²


13m²=5²+y²


Step 2


Subtract the leg from both sides


13m²=5²+y²


-5² -5²


Step 3


Square root the difference.


√144=√y


y=12



Solve for m


Step 1


Square the hypothenuse and leg.


c²=a²+b²


5000m²=3000²+m²


Step 2


Subtract the leg from both sides

-3000² -3000²

Square root the difference.


√16,000,000=√m²
